Elevator Pitch


We help agents find products you love


AI agents are starting to purchase things on our behalf. The trillion-dollar industries that never really worked - advertising, paid promotion, online reviews, SEO/GEO - will collapse entirely. Agents can't tell good products from well-marketed ones. They need ground truth - and whoever owns it owns the next economy.


We're building the largest network of human evaluators on earth. They receive products, earn by reviewing them honestly, and generate structured product data that agents can trust. Every agent shopping with our data pays us. In one week, we’ve onboarded 50 evaluators and 7 brands with a combined valuation of $300M.
Social media creators currently serve as our human evaluators. They receive products from partnered brands, review them, and make content if they love them, earning through affiliate commissions. Brands get authentic content promoting their product. Creators get free samples and only promote them if they believe in them. We take a cut of every sale.
The future of agentic commerce will run on Giftly.

Why did we decide to pivot? Why are we working on this?


Samarjit and I both suffer from seborrheic dermatitis - commonly known as… dandruff. Working long, stressful hours, we have the worst flare-ups at night. One night, working on Mandarins (our previous idea), we were debating dandruff shampoos and generally discussing how hard it is to find something that specifically works for us. I’ve personally spent over 60$ buying dandruff shampoo online on Amazon, and still haven’t found something that works for me. 


While this is a specific use case, we got to talking about how these products even reach us anyway. I found most of my products based on what Amazon floated to the top, and we thought this whole process was a bit shady. We came up with a side project idea: a platform that replaces paid collaborations between brands and creators with string-free product gifting, where the creators only make content if they genuinely like and would use the product. We thought this would be better for everyone, so we talked to 10-15 creators about the idea over the span of a couple weeks.


A week after launching Giftly, user conversations turned into 50 creators and 7 DTC brands with around $300M total valuation on board. After spending more time just thinking about the idea and AI, we saw a future where it exploded.

Why now?


Agent-driven shopping has just started to ship (OpenAI Instant Checkout, Operator, Perplexity Shopping, Rufus, Claude+MCP, Gemini). ChatGPT now processes 50 million shopping queries a day. Over a million Shopify merchants are onboarding right now - Glossier, SKIMS, Spanx, Vuori. The infrastructure for buying is built - AGP, UCP, AP2, MCP, A2A. 
  
  



The infrastructure for choosing well isn’t. Thirty percent of online reviews are fake, and this number is growing with increased AI bot spam. [Segment on why reviews are fucked]. Amazon spent $500 million and 8,000 employees on this last year and still let 275 million fake reviews through. Columbia and Yale published a paper last August showing that different AI agents pick different products from the same query, and that sellers making minor tweaks to product descriptions to target AI buyers already see measurable market-share gains. The agents are reading from poisoned data, and the pollution compounds the moment it gets summarized into a single confident recommendation that millions of agents act on. Eighty-three percent of consumers say they don't trust AI shopping agents yet, and they're right not to. 


With agentic commerce projected to be valued at $3-5 trillion dollars by 2030, whoever owns the signal will own the next economy. Giftly is that opportunity.


  





Why can’t other competitors do what we’re doing?


Why OpenAI and Anthropic won't do this. Because they've already said they won't, and we should take them at their word. OpenAI's exact language on Instant Checkout is that product results are "organic and unsponsored, ranked purely on relevance," and that merchant payments "don't influence ChatGPT's product results." They cannot also be the trust signal. The moment they pick winners, they lose neutrality, and neutrality is the entire reason people trust ChatGPT to shop. They need an external source of ground truth - and right now they're scraping it from Wirecutter and Reddit, which is exactly the polluted data we started Giftly to replace. We're not their competitor. We’re infrastructure that is missing.


Other companies would have to completely uproot their structure, build a network of people and brands, and establish trust. Giftly’s pipeline is not simply a feature; it's an entire company, and it’s hard for me to believe that a company that is doing well already would completely devour itself to implement what we’ve been doing. 




Why are we the ones to do this? What is different about us?


We don’t have an attention economics background. We care about AI capabilities and reasoning.


Our last year has been spent building around and researching AI agents. For Mandarins and ARC, we were building harnesses. In our personal research, we’ve analyzed human-AI interaction, reverse-engineered decision-making with Bayesian inverse planning, and have dedicated ourselves to answering how agents can make good decisions from limited, noisy human input. We have a toolkit that most people in this space don’t know exists.


Also, the proof is in the pudding. We got 50 creators and 7 DTC brands totaling $300 million in valuation onto the platform in a week, from cold outreach alone. The reason this works is that there’s a gap for all parties.

The 5-year arc
Mercor built the human-expertise layer that frontier AI labs run on for capability training. Giftly is building the human-trust layer that frontier AI agents run on for commerce decisions. Same shape — humans as infrastructure for AI — but a different customer, a different signal, and a market an order of magnitude larger.
By year 5, every brand selling through agent surfaces is on Giftly because invisibility means non-existence in agent-mediated commerce. Every human with verifiable expertise — creators, professionals, domain experts, verified buyers — is enlistable as an evaluator with auditable provenance. The signal we generate is the default trust layer the labs query when ranking products, the way Stripe became the default payment layer the labs query when transacting.
Three-layer agent commerce stack: transaction (Stripe), identity (Cloudflare + labs), trust (Giftly). Mercor for trust. $5T market by 2030.
